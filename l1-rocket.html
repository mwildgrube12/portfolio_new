L1 Rocket

Overview

      I am building a rocket as a part of the rocketry project team in the Tufts University SEDS chapter with the goal of obtaining an L1 certification by the NAR.  ​​

Part 1: ​Design

       I started by designing the rocket in OpenRocket to get a basic idea of what shape fins and what weight everything would need in order to safely fly to a height of 700-900 meters. In order for it to be stable, I needed to have a center of pressure that was about 2 body tube diameters behind the I made large tapered fins so that the center of pressure was pulled farther back, and put holes in the non-load-bearing centering rings  to reduce the weight of the assembly. 
         I then modeled the rocket parts that I needed in OnShape, and laser cut and 3d print the parts I needed. I adjusted some things like the infill percentage of the nose cone as I got more accurate weights to keep the rocket stable. Then, I did provisionary assembly to check how it all fit.​​

Part 2: Design, Part 2: Payload and Rocketry Lead

       Originally, the launch was going to be in late November, but due to scheduling issues, it had to be delayed until January, and then until April. Indirectly because of the delays and various other reasons, I took over as rocketry co-lead in late January and early February and began to run rocketry. While I was helping out everyone else design their rockets for the new launch date, I took the liberty of designing a payload nose cone for flight data (though I didn't end up getting it because of failures of the altimeter). I designed it so that the nose cone could screw off and put stuff inside, and have holes so that the pressure could stabilize. During this time, I also planned out a schedule for everyone else to make a rocket in 2.5 months

Part 3: Launch

        In late April, I was finally able to launch my rocket with the rest of the rocketry team. Though we only got through my rocket and the other lead's rocket because a wildfire got started, we were finally able to see the rockets we made fly. It was decided that my rocket only sustained minor damage on the flight, and I was awarded an L1 certification.
