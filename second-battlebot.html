Second Battlebot

Overview

      During the 2022 fall, I improved upon my previous years robot by using the same top spinner idea and improving it with metal parts and more sturdy structures, while still keeping it under the weight limit​​

​Design Considerations and Build

       Last year, I made the battle bot almost completely out of acrylic, with only small wood and metal plates with nails on the outside. In addition, the parts were only attached by brittle 3d printed hinges, which did not provide proper support. Instead, with my team of 1 computer scientist and 1 electrical engineering student, to build a completely metal outer shell this time. The design consisted of a circular acrylic plate as a base for the electronics and gearbox to sit on and a outer shell that was supposed to spin while the bot moved around.
         To improve saftey, this year we were required to have kill switches on the outside. Since my entire body was going to be spinning, I ended up having to make specialized axels and screws to fit wires through. Using a lathe, I made an alluminum hollow axel and threaded it to fit a custom hollow screw on the bottom and a bolt to hold it on the top. I then desined a 3d printed gearbox to hold it and attached a bearing on the bottom so it span better.​​

Design difficulties

       I had slight problems making the gearbox, and had to make last minute adjustments to get the tolerances right because they weren't perfect enough. This was due to the gearbox being plastic and the top being heavy and hard to spin, as well as limitations of 3d printing
       I also had to go through multiple iterations with the axel, as the original material, copper, was too soft and didn't hold the screws well enough. This forced me to make the axel from scrach and the screw from scratch, inlucing tuning it to the right diameter and drilling the hole in the center with the lathe, threading with dies and with the lathe, and putting the grips on the screw.

Competition

         When the competition went, since we went up close to the dealine due to electronics issues, the gearbox ended up having a malfunction and the belt came slighly loose by a few millimeters and ended up not spinning the top. Despite that, we ran the robot in the competition but didn't due well due to code issues making it hard to drive. Our robot got burned by a flame robot
