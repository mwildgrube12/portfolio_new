Battlebot

Overview

      With a partner, I am designing a battlebot to compete in the robotics club's intra-club BattleBot competition. I am doing all of the structural parts and most of the electrical stuff while he is doing most of the coding for the project​​

​Design Considerations and Build

       I started the project by design ing the robot structure in OnShape and laser cut the sides in cast acrylic. I 3d-printed hinges to hold it all together. The design uses a pyramid to attempt to reduce direct forces to the sides and protect the bot from being flipped. The robot uses an arduino to mediate between a RC controller and the motor shield for the bot to drive. The arduino also mediates control between the rc controller and the weapon motor, which is a 12v brushless motor meant to spin a weapon on the top. The weapon is a cross shaped spinner on the top that comes down the sides with sharpened nails, meant to scratch and eventually fracture at the acrylic of the other bots (most of the other students are also freshman unable to machine metal for their bot due to restrictions at the time on the machine shop usage).​​

Design difficulties

       There was some difficulties in the design of the robot. Originally, the bit was supposed to be built by a team of 5-6; however, many of the kids dropped out before actual progress on the bot was made due to other responsibilities. This left the entirety of the structure design on me to figure out for myself. The biggest troubles I had include:
  • sizing the robot with weird angles from CAD to real life
    • ​fixed by resizing and learning not to use hard dimensions
  • fitting the weapon with the nails without shattering acrylic
    • fixed by switching weapon sides to wood
  •  ensuring the hinges don't snap
    • ​used 80% infill to make hinges stronger
Although I would have rather done it with a group, doing the structure alone has taught me much about manufacturing a machine.

Competition

         The competition went decently well. The robot worked; however, an unknown issue occurred during the first battle that made the bot loose connection to the controller and spin out of control. In addition, after control was lost, part of the weapon snapped off because the hinges were 3d printed. Overall, even though the bot didn't do the best, I think the project was a success from what I learned from it.
