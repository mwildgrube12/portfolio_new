3G 38mm Rocket 

Overview

      Before I worked on getting my L2 rocket in the winter, I decided to design another roket around a a extra motor we had, since I wanted to launch while helping the other members design and launch their L1 rockets.​​

Part 1: ​Design

       The main challenge of this rocket was originally I had a week to design a rocket from scratch so that I could launch that weekend. Because of this, I reused the things that work during my first launch, while adapting them to a new motor and design. I made a similar fin assembly, where I mostly just changed the centering rings to fit the new motor diameter, bread length to fit the motor casing length, changed the fins to be smaller because the old fins didn't need to be that big, and changed how it connected to be simpler for assemble in the limited time. I reused the old design of the nose almost completely because there were little problems with it and the rocket would undergo similar conditions. Though the flight that day ended up being cancelled, I had most the rocket ready to launch aside from final assembly pre-launch​​

Part 3: Launch

        The launch ended up being delayed twice due to weather; however, we ended up launching in late october. I ended up launching last after aiding 11 people launch L1 attempts, around 8 of which were successful. Our one altimeter had broken on an earlier flight on a nose cone ejection failure, so i had to launch without it. On site we learned some helpful tips and epoxied our fin assemblies to the body tube to make them more secure. This launch for me, however, ended up having an unknown parachute deployment failure, with the 2 possible caused being the launch cord getting tangles and the jolly logic failing. However, I learned how to better make my design so that my L2 attempt will have a higher rate of success.
