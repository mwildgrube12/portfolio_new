Past Science Fair Projects

Overview

     I did science fair for much of my childhood, and here are some overviews of what I did in those years along with pictures of the things that I made and tested​​

Wind tunnel pt 1: 3d printed propellers for wind energy

        For this project, I tested the effectiveness of various cambered airfoils for effectiveness in wind energy applications in a homemade cardboard wind tunnel that I designed. Used propellers that were 3D printed using selective laser sintering (SLS) attached to a repurposed fan motor to measure voltage output when put in the windtunnel. Won first place at Bucks County Science Research Competition (BCSRC), 1st award at PJAS regional competition, and 2nd award at PJAS state competition. Qualified for Delaware Valley Science Fair.​​

Wind tunnel pt 2: 3d printed wings

     I used my wind tunnel for a second year, this time to test the effectiveness of various airfoils to improve the lift of wings. I improved upon the design by using a leaf blower instead of a 20 inch fan and implementing flow splitters to attempt to create less turbulent flow. The wings were 3d printed using SLS again this year. The project won first place at BCSRC, 1st award at PJAS regional competition, and 2nd award at PJAS state competition. Qualified for Delaware Valley Science Fair.​​

Corrosion Testing

     Completed testing on various metals on resistance to corrosion while bathed in water with the salinity of the ocean. Tested zinc, copper, and stainless steel in deionized water, 35 g/L saltwater (avg. ocean water), and 70 g/L saltwater. Measured every day for a month both qualitatively and quantitatively for corrosion. Won 2nd award at PJAS science fair ​​
