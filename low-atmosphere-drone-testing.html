12th grade research project:Low-atmosphere drone testing

Overview

     I completed a year-long research project through my high school's stem research program. I studied creating an unmanned quadcopter to fly on Mars. To do this, I did testing on propellers in less atmosphere with a bell vacuum chamber. After, I wrote a journal-style paper on my research, found below. ​​

Part 1: ​Propeller Spacing Tests

       Initially, I was going to test spacing between two different propellers. However, I changed focus from this problem due problems with oscillations from a 3d printed gearbox, and inability to machine one. Below are a few pictures from the first stage of testing​​

Part 2: Propeller length test + Vacuum chamber tests

       Eventually, in interest of getting to do tests inside the vacuum chamber, I switched focus to test different sized individual propellers. I 3D-printed 4 different sized propellers and tested them in both at sea-level pressure and in reduced pressure inside a vacuum chamber. I then wrote the culminating paper on the project
Electronics for remote control in vacuum chamber
One propeller in Blender

Reflection

        Throughout this year, my engineering goal was to create a drone that could fly on Mars, or to create design guidelines for such a drone. Neither of these goals were achieved due to many problems encountered while designing the electronics and the gearbox for the testing apparatus. However, I did learn much through the process. My knowledge of some scientific concepts and computer science improved. I learned better how electronic circuits work, improved my abilities to code by using the arduino, and improved my 3d modeling skill so I can better design something in the future. I also learned about the difficulties of research projects and learned much about the process.
