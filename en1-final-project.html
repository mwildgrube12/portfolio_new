Robotics Final Project

Overview

     For my engineering class, we designed a virtually controlled robot for the Horace Man School for the deaf. In a group of 4, I helped create a robotic shuffleboard game using robotic shooters and automatic scoring and designed to be operated over the internet. My part included helping design the shooting mechanism and manufacturing the board.​​

Prototyping 

     I did many layers of prototyping for this project. Before we had to turn in a final prototype, my group member and I went through many iterations for the first presentation of our project. First, we exchanged ideas using Onshape CAD designs, then, we made multiple models using laser cut wood. Thought it was emitted from the final version due to worries it would confuse the kids among other issues, I also tried to create a side to side gearbox. Finally, for the prototype presentation, I also fashioned a simple dry-erase board with walls to serve as the shuffle bard itself. After this, we did a prototype presentation of our current version.​​

Final version

     We changed many aspects of our project form the prototype version to the final version to improve reliability and to polish some looks and features. I improved the frame to be more sturdy and helped iron out some issues with the shooters. We were unable to present it in the end due to Covid restrictions by the university; we were able to make a fully working version in the end.​​
